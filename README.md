# Florida-Retail-Sales-Weather-Impact-Analysis-Power-BI-
End-to-end Power BI analysis of retail performance across four Florida cities, uncovering how weather patterns, customer segments, and seasonality influence sales, with actionable insights for revenue optimization and strategic decision-making.
This project presents a data-driven analysis of retail shop performance across four key locations in Florida: Miami Beach, Orlando, Tampa, and Jacksonville.

The goal was to uncover how external factors (weather), customer segmentation, and seasonal patterns influence sales performance, and to translate those findings into actionable business recommendations.

🎯 Business Questions

The dashboard was designed to answer the following:

How strongly do temperature and rainfall affect daily sales?
Which shop performs best and why?
Who are the key customers (Singles vs Families, Male vs Female)?
Are there predictable seasonal patterns in sales?
What actions should be taken based on these insights?

🧠 Key Insights
Weather Impact
Sales show a clear positive relationship with temperature trends, suggesting increased consumer activity during warmer periods
Rainfall likely introduces short-term volatility in daily sales
Top Performing Location
Miami Beach leads in average sales, indicating stronger demand or better market positioning
Customer Composition
Slight dominance of female customers (~51%)
Singles contribute marginally more than families, suggesting different purchasing behavior patterns
Seasonality
Sales peak mid-year and towards year-end, indicating predictable seasonal demand cycles

🚀 Business Recommendations
Align promotions and inventory with high-temperature peak periods
Increase marketing efforts in lower-performing cities (e.g., Jacksonville)
Tailor product offerings toward single buyers, while creating bundled offers to attract families
Prepare operational scaling (staffing, stock) ahead of seasonal peaks
Leverage weather forecasts for short-term sales planning
